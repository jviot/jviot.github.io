### Background
As part of my projects, I have developed expertise in several fields, detailed through vignettes. **[This first vignette focuses on liver metastases](#)**.

The liver exhibits a **unique immune microenvironment**, marked by heightened tolerance due to constant exposure to gut-derived pathogens via the portal circulation. This environment promotes tumor growth, making the liver a key site for metastasis.

In **colorectal cancers**, the liver is the **primary site of metastatic spread**, acting as a filter in the portal circulation and representing a critical step in hematogenous progression.

---

### Research Work
1. **Study of lymphocytes infiltrating liver metastases** :
   I investigated the impact of lymphocytes (subtype expressing **CD226**), associated with activated cytotoxic T cells. These are linked to **improved tumor control** and **prolonged survival** in operated patients.
   **Publication** : [PubMed (36717657)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36717657/) (medical thesis).

2. **Endogenous retrovirus expression** :
   Identified loci associated with endogenous retroviruses, showing **prognostic value** in patients.
   **Publication** : [PubMed (40381378)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40381378/) (PhD thesis).

---

### Methodology
- **Bioinformatics analyses** : **Bulk RNA-seq** and **single-cell RNA-seq** samples (primary colorectal cancers and matched liver metastases).
- **Biological validation** : Flow cytometry, IHC, PCR.
- **Outlook** : Some findings remain exploratory and require further investigation.
